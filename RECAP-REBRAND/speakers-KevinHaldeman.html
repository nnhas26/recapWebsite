VOL.1 - Building A Resilient Team Structure

Summary: In this webinar session, Anne explained how to form a resilient team in the current pandemic situation. Before getting into the main points in making a resilient team, Anne talked about her experiences 11 years ago, when she was still at Google, and at that time the world was being hit by a global financial crisis. The condition where stock prices in Europe and the world were falling again and again. Anne was required to reorganize the recruitment process at Google, and she had to cut 50% of the number of people who will be recruited. In other words, at that time Google needed 1000 people to grow its business, but because of the crisis, Google could only accept 500 people for the job.

At that time, Google tried to create a strong team, by maximizing the potential of the team members and the people who had been recruited until the business conditions recovered. Of course, it is not easy to do it all, where people's motivation and productivity have decreased due to the crisis. Instead of focusing on large recruitment every year, Anne learned a lesson at Google that it is better to focus on building a strong team by holding internal team training as well as building a pipeline of products that will be launched in the future. Flexibility is needed within the team because basically the crisis is unpredictable. Here's what you need to do to build a strong team:

The first point is to create a High Level of Collaboration
In order for all team members to be collaborative, it must create a sense of mutual trust by not only involving them in their daily routine work but also in new projects, where they can give ideas and take their respective parts. The point is, during a pandemic like this you have to think about other things you can do from your home and create something new.
The second point is to set a clear goal

The second point after creating a collaborative team, as a founder or leader in a business, a team must set a clear goal. Everyone must clearly understand what they should do, the scope of their responsibilities, and why they should do things.

Anne provides analogies with people in the world of aviation. A pilot's job is to make sure every passenger and the contents inside the plane are safe. Besides the pilot, there is also an air traffic controller who alerts you if there are any weather-related problems. Information from air traffic controllers is what will be considered by the pilots to make a decision to fly and land the plane. In this case, everyone has their respective duties, and each of them knows the importance of their role.

It's the same in business. If the air traffic controller does not understand their duties or does not work properly, the result is chaotic air traffic, which will harm not only the company but also the crowd.

Therefore, it is important to understand the role of each person on the team and why they are so important in this team. In a pandemic like this, the leader has to explain what are the goals of the company and what everyone should do. Then, added several measuring tools that must be followed by all team members.

The third point is the willingness to improve

In crisis conditions, it takes a lot of out of the box thinking from each team member. Anne provides an example of her experience at Google. At Google, there is a culture where 20% of the time should be spent working on a project. With this principle, employees can spend as much as 20% of their time & energy outside of their daily work, to create personal or side projects.

One thing that needs to be done is finding out new and unique ideas in a team. One thing to remember is don't be afraid to make mistakes. Because the most important thing from this improvisation is to learn from the experiences.

The last point is to create psychological safety

To be innovative, it is essential to ensure the psychological safety of all team members. All team members must be comfortable with their opinions. It doesn't always have to be crazy ideas to create innovation. It could be, a simple idea from a team member turns out to be exactly what many people need, which ultimately keeps the company going. This is where the Founders' job is to listen and accommodate their ideas.
