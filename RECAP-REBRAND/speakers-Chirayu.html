VOL. 2 - Fundraising Post-Pandemic: Start Now

Summary: Covid-19 is significantly affects all types of businesses including service sector businesses, MSMEs, startups, also large companies. In addition, Covid-19 also makes entrepreneurs who want to build a business feel anxious. Of course, capital does not only come from personal expenses, it can also come from investors. But when everything is in trouble during the pandemic, do investors still want to provide funds for entrepreneurs?

Chirayu discussed this topic from a fairly broad perspective so that it can be studied for entrepreneurs who are just starting a business. According to Chirayu, the Co-Founder in the early days of the business was not a determining factor in obtaining funds from the investors. The main things that are considered by the investors are business projections, who is the target, and things that can be developed in the future. Before seeking funds from investors, entrepreneurs can do bootstrapping or use their Founders’ capital. Then if you have received funds, the next step is to manage funds from the investors. Bootstrapping is very profitable in the early days of building a business. According to Chirayu, entrepreneurs will be more creative and quick to make decisions on what happens to their business.

For example, an entrepreneur is starting a K-POP custom fandom merchandise business by selling stickers, clothes, and key chains. It turns out that there are products that are stagnant in sales, entrepreneurs can immediately stop the production of these products and then use their capital for other products that are more desirable to customers. However, if you have investors, you need to have a discussion beforehand with preparation in the form of a presentation to make a decision. In addition, with their own capital, it will be easier for an entrepreneur to monitor and record their business growth because all capital and cash are from the founder’s. According to Chirayu, related to the current pandemic, there is no right time to start a business because basically an entrepreneur can see every opportunity from every situation, even in the most chaotic situations. With regard to funding, investors will pay more attention to the suitability of businesses in the current pandemic. According to Chirayu, investors will consider funding a business based on business adaptation to current conditions, steps to be taken, or strategies to deal with problems arising from this pandemic.


Date: 22nd August 2020
Speaker: Chirayu Wadke
